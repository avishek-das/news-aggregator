It takes a lot of time reading and understanding about AI but often I feel like there are a lot of developments happening in AI which I cannot keep track of.

Field is evolving so fast with new research papers, new GitHub repositories, new products, new large language models and other models being released on a weekly basis. It feels like even if you are not keeping track for two weeks, you are falling behind a lot.

On top of things it's impossible for a human to look at every single platform. You could look at x.com or LinkedIn. There are a lot of interesting subjects, interesting research papers, preprints on arXIv, Conference Publications on top-tier conferences and then there are a lot of people. There are a lot of researchers constantly coming up with new ideas and new things happening in AI and there are these big companies always giving updates, OpenAI and Anthropic, et cetera.

Now what I want to do is build a website. This website should serve as a single platform for me to keep track of all the things that are happening in AI. When I say all the things happening in AI, I mean things that I find relevant. So here are some things that I have written down which I want to track -

- People
- Companies
- Research papers
- GitHub repos
- x.com
- LinkedIn
- Reddit
- Top researchers
- Conferences - ICML, NeurIPS, etc.
- Products
- LLM models

If there's anything that I'm missing, let me know. I'll be more than happy to add.

I want you to do the implementation in stages. You should never ever implement before planning so this planning should be done in versions. You should create a project roadmap document. You should work in development and then later work on production.

If I feel like the output that the website is showing me is not good, there should be a feedback system where I can keep on giving my preferences.
Based on those preferences, the website should show me things that I like more. This can be a very simple system, a thumbs up, thumbs down system, but all of these details we can discuss later in the development stage.
Initially, planning to make this website a single-user website is just for me. Authentication is also not necessarily needed.
In the later stage I want the user authentication so that individual people can use the website, store their preferences, and based on the preferences the content shown on the website will be modified.
Another point about website refreshing: how often should the information displayed on the website get refreshed? May be once a day, may be once in two days, depending on the plan that we make.
